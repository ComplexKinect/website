Team Learning

Make thoughtful, detailed, and specific decisions and create a timeline based on those decisions.

During our sprint four retrospective we met as a whole PoE team and reflected on our PoE experience as a whole. We all came to the conclusion that during this project as a team we made a lot of mistakes which hurt our ability to efficiently work towards our final product, but did result in a lot of positive learning.

Retrospectively, we see our biggest pitfall as how we began each sprint with. We approached each sprint with a stressed mindset driven by the time crunch which resulted in making decisions before we spent enough time thinking about them. This stressed mindset early in the design process created a positive feedback loop where we rushed the design process which created poor designs, which we then had to rework.

The driving factor behind our stressed mindset was our teams inability to make decisions early in the project. We struggled as a team to make decisions early in the project because we were frightened by the commitment that those decisions would signify. Our team fear of commitment meant that we didnâ€™t have a solid design idea until the second sprint which created what felt like a time crunch causing the stress driving our positive feedback loop.

Everything is always clearer after the fact, and looking back on this project we have all learned the importance of making decisions and looking at the project as one entity rather than four smaller entities. Moving forward, we are all going to take the lessons learned from the mistakes we made in this project and apply that learning to all of the future projects we will work on at Olin and beyond.
